# cert-suggester
> Tells you which security certification to pursue — and in what order — for your goals.

## Description
A needs-based advisor that recommends the right security certification(s) and a sequenced roadmap from your goal, experience level, domain interest, budget, timeline, and hands-on-vs-theory preference. It catalogs ~50 certs across offensive, blue/SOC, cloud, GRC, and foundational domains — with durable metadata and links to the official exam pages for the details that change. Honest by design: needs-based (no vendor bias), no pass/job guarantees, no braindumps.

## Compatibility
Advisory skill — no tooling or runtime required. Exam details (cost, dates, passing scores, exam codes) change; the skill gives durable structure and points to the official exam page for live specifics — always verify there before committing.

## Keywords
which certification should I get, security certification advisor, cert recommendation, OSCP vs CEH, CISSP, Security+, CompTIA, GIAC, SANS, AWS security cert, Azure security, cybersecurity career path, certification roadmap, pentest cert, blue team cert, SOC cert, GRC cert, cloud security cert, beginner cybersecurity certification

## Sample input
> "I'm a junior sysadmin with ~1 year experience. I want to get into pentesting, I have about $1k and 6 months, and I prefer hands-on over theory. Which certs should I get, and in what order?"

## Sample output
*(Illustrative shape.)*
- **Recommended path:** Security+ (baseline, DoD-recognized) → eJPT (first hands-on offensive cert, no prereqs) → PNPT or OSCP+ next.
- **Why:** matches your entry level, hands-on preference, budget tier, and 6-month window; avoids jumping straight to OSCP without fundamentals.
- **Alternatives:** CPTS as an OSCP+ peer; PNPT if you want a lower-cost hands-on exam with a debrief.
- **Each cert:** issuer · level · format · what it proves · official link to verify current cost/date.

## Known limitations
- **Advisory only — it does not teach the cert content or exam material** (that's separate prep skills) and it does not run anything.
- **No guarantees:** it can't promise a pass, a job, or a salary; outcomes depend on study effort, employer preferences, and market conditions.
- **Volatile details are pointed-to, not asserted:** exact prices, dates, passing scores, and exam codes change — the skill links the official page and tells you to verify; a fast-moving cert may have changed since the catalog was written.
- **No braindumps / leaked exam content** — it recommends legitimate study paths only.
- Catalog focuses on security certifications; general IT/PM certs are out of scope.
